Understanding Self-Esteem & Imposter Syndrome

What is IT?

Self-esteem is how we see and value ourselves. When it's low, we may feel unsure or unworthy.

Impostor syndrome is the persistent belief that we're not as capable as others think—even if there's plenty of proof otherwise.

What causes IT?

  • Perfectionism
  • Constant comparisons
  • Past experiences (criticism, bullying)
  • Cultural or family pressure
  • High academic or career expectations

Social media often adds pressure by showing only the best parts of others' lives.

What you can do

  • Reframe negative thoughts ("Would I say this to a friend?")
  • Talk to someone you trust
  • Practice small acts of self-kindness
  • Set doable goals
  • Reach out to a counselor, therapist, or mentor
  • Try resilience workshops or self-worth coaching

Facts and myths

Myth: Only insecure people experience impostor syndrome.
Fact: Even high achievers feel like impostors sometimes.
Myth: If you have low self-esteem, you're stuck with it forever.
Fact: Self-esteem can grow and shift over time.
